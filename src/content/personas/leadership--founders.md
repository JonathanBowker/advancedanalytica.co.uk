# Eleanor Price

Choose an entry point that creates reusable business value rather than isolated AI experiments that cannot scale with control. For founders, the key is building an operating asset that compounds across the business instead of a series of disconnected tooling bets.

## What This Persona Is Trying To Achieve

- Choose a starting point that creates reusable business value quickly.
- Connect knowledge, delivery, and governance in one operating model.
- Scale across functions without losing control or clarity.

## Where Pressure Shows Up

- Tool purchases and pilots create activity but not a coherent operating model.
- Different functions move at different speeds and define AI value differently.
- Governance, delivery, and strategy are often treated as separate workstreams.

## How This Persona Measures Success

- Time to first credible business outcome from a governed starting point.
- Reuse of knowledge assets and operating logic across functions.
- Reduction in fragmented AI initiatives and duplicated effort.
- Greater confidence in scaling AI without loss of control or visibility.

## Questions This Persona Is Likely To Ask

- Where should we start first to create the strongest operational leverage?
- How do we turn business knowledge into a reusable strategic asset?
- What role does AICE play in scaling safely across functions?
- How do we avoid disconnected pilots and create one governed path to scale?

## Likely Objections

- This may sound like a long operating-model programme before value is visible.
- Functions may resist a shared framework if it feels too centralised.
- Leadership teams may want faster proof before backing structural work.
- There may be uncertainty about where to begin and who should lead.

## Stakeholders This Persona Works With

- Founders and executives
- Transformation leaders
- Strategy leads
- Programme sponsors
- Function heads across brand, operations, product, and governance
- Finance, risk, and technical leadership

## Proof This Persona Needs

- Examples of one function becoming the launch point for wider operating capability.
- Evidence that governance and delivery discipline create leverage rather than friction.
- Clear explanation of how iBOM and AICE support scale, not just pilots.

## Content That Helps At Each Stage

- Awareness: why tool purchases do not create durable operating capability.
- Evaluation: how to choose the right business entry point and sequence delivery.
- Implementation: connecting function-level rollout to shared governance and infrastructure.
- Scale: extending the same operating logic across multiple functions and systems.

## Source Feeds To Watch

- Executive AI strategy newsletters.
- Operating model, transformation, and enterprise architecture feeds.
- Market signals on AI adoption, governance, and business capability building.

## Reference Content To Reuse

- Past content on what-is-iBOM, operating model themes, and multi-function governance.
- Use cases that show one function leading into a broader rollout path.
- Articles and pages that convert well for strategic readers.

## Topics Most Likely To Perform

- Why tools do not equal capability.
- Choosing the right AI starting point.
- Knowledge as an owned strategic asset.
- Scaling with governance rather than chaos.

## Engagement Signals To Watch

- Good engagement on strategic explainers that stay concrete.
- Strong CTA performance on pages linking one function to a wider journey.
- Better retention when commercial language is tied to operating discipline.
- More return traffic from content offering a coherent programme view.

## Preferred Formats

- Executive explainers.
- Decision frameworks.
- Journey articles mapping from pilot to scale.
- Thought leadership grounded in operating model logic.

## Editorial Voice

- Strategic but still operational.
- Commercially literate and confident.
- Less technical detail, more sequencing and leverage.
- Clear enough to support decisions, not just awareness.

## Content Objectives

- Help leadership readers move from scattered AI interest to operating-model thinking.
- Create confidence in a phased but ambitious route to scale.
- Support commercial conversations with content that feels strategic and credible.

## CTA Patterns

- Open with fragmentation or pilot fatigue, then show the alternative.
- Use one function as a concrete entry point before zooming out.
- End with a clear journey CTA, not a vague invitation.

## Avoid Patterns

- Avoid tool-by-tool comparison content.
- Do not drift into technical detail too early.
- Avoid fluffy transformation rhetoric with no operating logic behind it.

## Update Cadence

Review weekly for strategic AI and transformation signals, and monthly for which leadership-oriented content is driving the strongest downstream engagement.

## Content That Helps

- Commercial narratives that connect AI investment to operating capability.
- Examples of phased rollout from one function into wider adoption.
- Clear explanation of how iBOM and AICE work together at programme level.

## Messaging Notes

- Responds to coherence, leverage, and strategic reuse.
- Needs confidence that governance and speed can reinforce each other.
- Cares about operating model design more than tool comparisons.
