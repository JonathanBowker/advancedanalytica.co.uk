# David Ng

Create a more testable and traceable operating model for AI systems across workflows, agents, and internal tools. The aim is to make governance part of how systems operate day to day, not just a separate oversight layer sitting outside delivery.

## What This Persona Is Trying To Achieve

- Make policy operational before issues reach live systems.
- Reduce uncontrolled model behaviour, unmanaged access, and policy drift.
- Create stronger traceability, assurance, and runtime enforcement.

## Where Pressure Shows Up

- Policies are often readable but not operational in live systems.
- Manual oversight alone does not scale as AI use expands.
- Runtime risk appears faster than traditional review processes can respond.

## How This Persona Measures Success

- Policy adherence and reduction in control breaches.
- Improved auditability and traceability of AI system behaviour.
- Reduction in unmanaged access and runtime exceptions.
- Faster response to policy change, drift, and control updates.

## Questions This Persona Is Likely To Ask

- How do we turn policy into something systems can actually follow?
- What does governed runtime enforcement look like in practice?
- How do we reduce live risk rather than only reviewing it after the fact?
- How do we maintain traceability as rules, models, and obligations evolve?

## Likely Objections

- This sounds technically ambitious for teams used to policy documentation, not system design.
- Human oversight will still be needed, so why formalise this much?
- The organisation may resist stronger runtime controls around existing workflows.
- The AI landscape is moving too fast to operationalise every policy requirement.

## Stakeholders This Persona Works With

- Legal counsel
- Risk leadership
- Compliance teams
- Policy owners
- Governance leads
- Engineering, security, and executive stakeholders

## Proof This Persona Needs

- Examples of policy turning into machine-usable logic and live enforcement.
- Evidence that runtime control reduces real exposure rather than just documenting risk.
- Practical assurance patterns for traceability, review, and revision.

## Content That Helps At Each Stage

- Awareness: why policy on paper is not enough for live AI systems.
- Evaluation: what rules, controls, and approvals must become operational first.
- Implementation: structuring policy, deploying AICE, and governing runtime behaviour.
- Scale: auditability, revision, and enforcement across more systems and teams.

## Source Feeds To Watch

- AI governance, policy, and compliance RSS feeds.
- Risk, audit, and regulatory newsletters.
- Standards and oversight updates related to AI control, safety, and assurance.

## Reference Content To Reuse

- Past content on governance guardrails, policy-as-graph, and guidelines-to-policy.
- Use cases around integrity, compliance, assurance, and governed agent systems.
- Articles with strong engagement from governance-minded readers.

## Topics Most Likely To Perform

- Policy to runtime enforcement.
- AI governance and guardrails.
- Auditability and traceability.
- Reducing live risk in AI systems.

## Engagement Signals To Watch

- Higher completion on practical policy and control explainers.
- Strong CTA performance when content connects governance to real operating exposure.
- Repeat visits to articles on enforcement, assurance, and runtime controls.
- Better engagement from content with concrete examples than broad ethics commentary.

## Preferred Formats

- Control frameworks.
- Governance explainers with operational examples.
- Short rebuttal articles handling common objections.
- FAQ-style pieces around assurance, policy, and live enforcement.

## Editorial Voice

- Measured, authoritative, and operational.
- Clear about risk without leaning on fear.
- Focused on enforceability and traceability.
- Professional and credible to governance audiences.

## Content Objectives

- Help readers see why documentation alone is insufficient.
- Position AICE and structured policy as the route to enforceable operational control.
- Generate content that can support both governance education and buying confidence.

## CTA Patterns

- Lead with live exposure or policy drift, not with abstract AI hype.
- Connect structured logic directly to runtime behaviour and reviewability.
- End with a function or role path that feels operational, not promotional.

## Avoid Patterns

- Avoid ethics platitudes with no operational path.
- Do not imply governance removes the need for human judgement entirely.
- Avoid overclaiming certainty in complex regulatory environments.

## Update Cadence

Review weekly against governance and regulatory feeds, and monthly against engagement signals from assurance, policy, and traceability content.

## Content That Helps

- Clear examples of policy becoming machine-usable operating logic.
- Explanations of how AICE enforces access, actions, and runtime constraints.
- Practical guidance on assurance, auditability, and policy revision.

## Messaging Notes

- Responds to control, traceability, and enforcement more than efficiency language.
- Needs proof that governance can shape design and operation, not just oversight.
- Cares about reducing live exposure through stronger operating discipline.
