# Olivia Mason

Build a governed content layer that helps AI-assisted production stay aligned across formats, channels, and internal teams. It creates a more durable operating asset for content creation, reuse, and quality assurance rather than leaving alignment to manual intervention every time.

## What This Persona Is Trying To Achieve

- Turn brand guidance into reusable operating assets rather than static documents.
- Make approvals, review logic, and execution standards easier to scale.
- Use AI without losing consistency, control, or traceability across channels.

## Where Pressure Shows Up

- Different teams interpret brand standards differently under delivery pressure.
- Approval cycles are slow, manual, and hard to scale across markets or channels.
- AI-assisted content creates speed, but also raises consistency and governance risk.

## How This Persona Measures Success

- Brand consistency across channels and markets.
- Approval cycle time and review throughput.
- Content reuse rate and production efficiency.
- Reduction in off-brand outputs from teams and AI systems.

## Questions This Persona Is Likely To Ask

- How do we make brand rules usable by AI systems rather than just readable by humans?
- What should be structured first: tone, examples, approvals, or policy logic?
- How do we keep agencies and internal teams working from the same operating model?
- How do we scale content production without weakening brand control?

## Likely Objections

- This sounds like a governance project that could slow marketing down.
- Our brand is too nuanced to be structured without losing judgement.
- Agencies and local teams will never work from one model.
- We already have brand guidelines, so why do we need another layer?

## Stakeholders This Persona Works With

- Brand leadership
- Marketing leadership
- Creative operations
- Content operations
- Agencies and external partners
- Product and service teams carrying the brand into customer channels

## Proof This Persona Needs

- Evidence that structured brand knowledge improves both speed and control.
- Examples of approval logic, policies, and standards becoming machine-usable.
- Proof that AI-assisted content can stay on-brand in live workflows.

## Content That Helps At Each Stage

- Awareness: why guidelines are not enough for AI-assisted execution.
- Evaluation: what to structure first and how to govern approvals.
- Implementation: building the brand knowledge layer and operating model.
- Scale: extending governed brand logic across channels, teams, and partners.

## Source Feeds To Watch

- Brand governance and content operations newsletters.
- Marketing operations, workflow, and approval-system RSS feeds.
- AI content assurance and brand safety updates.

## Reference Content To Reuse

- Past posts on brand-as-code, brand policy testing, and evaluating brand alignment.
- Existing use cases involving approval scale, global portfolio governance, and brand consistency.
- Historic articles that performed well around content operations and policy-to-execution themes.

## Topics Most Likely To Perform

- Brand governance in AI-assisted content.
- Approval logic at scale.
- Turning guidelines into operational systems.
- Maintaining consistency across channels and partners.

## Engagement Signals To Watch

- Long dwell time on operational explainers.
- Strong scroll depth on practical approval or governance frameworks.
- Higher click-through on content tying brand control to execution speed.
- Return visits to articles with concrete workflow examples.

## Preferred Formats

- Explainer articles with operational diagrams.
- Step-by-step implementation guides.
- Comparison pieces showing static guidance vs governed operating models.
- Short Q&A articles answering specific execution concerns.

## Editorial Voice

- Structured and operational.
- Concrete rather than inspirational.
- Confident about governance without sounding bureaucratic.
- Helpful to busy delivery-minded brand teams.

## Content Objectives

- Help readers recognise brand as an operating system problem, not just a guidelines problem.
- Move readers from abstract AI interest toward structured brand governance.
- Create trust that iBOM and AICE can improve both speed and control.

## CTA Patterns

- Begin with a governance or approval pain point, then move to structure and runtime control.
- Use concrete examples before introducing terminology.
- End with a next-step CTA tied to role or function journey pages.

## Avoid Patterns

- Do not sound like generic creative strategy advice.
- Avoid overclaiming that AI can replace brand judgement.
- Avoid vague transformation language without operational detail.

## Update Cadence

Review weekly against RSS and monthly against engagement trends to refresh topic priorities and winning article patterns.

## Content That Helps

- Examples of how brand knowledge can be structured into governed operating assets.
- Practical guidance on approvals, content assurance, and brand governance in AI workflows.
- Evidence that stronger structure improves consistency and operational speed together.

## Messaging Notes

- Responds to operational clarity more than abstract creativity language.
- Needs proof that governance can accelerate delivery rather than slow it down.
- Cares about practical rollout across channels, partners, and approval processes.
