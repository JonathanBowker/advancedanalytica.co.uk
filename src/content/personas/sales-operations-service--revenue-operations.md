# Priya Shah

Create one governed operating layer across CRM logic, handoffs, workflow automation, and operational reporting. The benefit is a more consistent way to structure operational rules and system behaviour so workflows, data handling, and AI-assisted actions stay aligned.

## What This Persona Is Trying To Achieve

- Reduce drift across operational processes and customer-facing workflows.
- Make AI useful inside live systems rather than as a disconnected assistant.
- Improve quality, speed, and consistency without losing oversight.

## Where Pressure Shows Up

- Operational rules live in people, spreadsheets, and local workarounds.
- Teams handle similar requests differently across channels and regions.
- AI support can create value quickly but also introduces inconsistency and control issues.

## How This Persona Measures Success

- Workflow completion time and handoff quality.
- Service quality and first-contact resolution.
- Operational consistency across teams and regions.
- Reduction in manual rework, escalation, and contradictory handling.

## Questions This Persona Is Likely To Ask

- How do we connect AI to operational systems without creating unmanaged behaviour?
- What workflow knowledge needs to be structured before automation becomes reliable?
- How do we reduce contradiction across sales, service, and delivery teams?
- What does quality assurance look like once AI is involved in live operations?

## Likely Objections

- This will be hard to integrate with existing CRM and service tooling.
- Operational edge cases are too messy to govern centrally.
- AI may speed things up, but it could increase risk in live service situations.
- Our teams will resist a new operating model if it feels imposed.

## Stakeholders This Persona Works With

- Sales leadership
- Revenue operations
- Service operations
- Support leadership
- Delivery management
- IT and systems owners for CRM, support, and workflow tooling

## Proof This Persona Needs

- Examples of AI working inside governed workflows, not just chat interfaces.
- Evidence that structured operational logic reduces drift and contradiction.
- Proof that runtime controls improve service quality rather than slow teams down.

## Content That Helps At Each Stage

- Awareness: why disconnected AI assistance does not fix workflow problems.
- Evaluation: where operational knowledge should be captured first.
- Implementation: connecting systems, rules, and AICE controls.
- Scale: monitoring quality and expanding governed automation across workflows.

## Source Feeds To Watch

- CRM, support automation, and RevOps RSS feeds.
- Service quality, CX operations, and workflow orchestration newsletters.
- AI operations and agent-control updates relevant to live service environments.

## Reference Content To Reuse

- Past articles on operations brand runtime, governance guardrails, and optimisation loops.
- Use cases involving operational assurance, workflow systems, and MCP guardrails.
- Previous content with strong engagement on live workflow control and service quality.

## Topics Most Likely To Perform

- Governed workflow automation.
- Reducing drift across service operations.
- AI in CRM and support environments.
- Assurance in live operational systems.

## Engagement Signals To Watch

- Strong scroll depth on articles with system and workflow examples.
- Higher CTA engagement on posts that connect AI to measurable service outcomes.
- Good retention on implementation-oriented pieces rather than general AI commentary.
- Repeat visits to content around QA, monitoring, and runtime controls.

## Preferred Formats

- Operational playbooks.
- Before-and-after workflow comparisons.
- Short diagnostic articles by problem type.
- Implementation checklists for live systems.

## Editorial Voice

- Practical, grounded, and operational.
- Focused on workflow realities and handoffs.
- Clear about risk without sounding alarmist.
- Useful for teams managing live service pressure.

## Content Objectives

- Help readers see why operational AI needs structure and runtime control.
- Show that AICE is relevant to systems and workflows, not just models.
- Drive readers from pain recognition into function and role journey pages.

## CTA Patterns

- Open with a service or workflow failure pattern.
- Tie governance directly to quality and throughput.
- End with the next relevant use case, role path, or contact CTA.

## Avoid Patterns

- Avoid generic productivity claims with no workflow context.
- Do not position AI as a detached assistant divorced from live systems.
- Avoid abstract transformation language without operational specifics.

## Update Cadence

Review weekly for new workflow and operations signals, and monthly for topic performance by format, CTA, and scroll behaviour.

## Content That Helps

- Operational examples that show AI working inside governed workflows.
- Guidance on structuring service logic, escalation rules, and handoffs.
- Proof that control, monitoring, and runtime assurance can coexist with speed.

## Messaging Notes

- Responds to practical workflow improvement over abstract transformation language.
- Needs operational credibility and clear examples tied to systems and handoffs.
- Cares about service quality, traceability, and repeatability.
