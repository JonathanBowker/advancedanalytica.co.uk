# Dr Hannah Lewis

Structure specialist insight so it can become a reusable operating asset instead of remaining trapped in projects and documents. This gives research leads a clearer path from valuable but isolated knowledge to governed systems and repeatable operational capability.

## What This Persona Is Trying To Achieve

- Turn specialist knowledge into reusable operational assets.
- Move from isolated experiments to governed delivery pathways.
- Create learning loops that preserve nuance while improving system behaviour over time.

## Where Pressure Shows Up

- High-value knowledge often remains trapped in people, reports, or prototype work.
- Promising experiments struggle to become reliable operating capability.
- Complex domains need stronger evidence, control, and revision than generic AI patterns provide.

## How This Persona Measures Success

- Time from research insight to usable operational asset.
- Quality of evaluation, evidence traceability, and revision loops.
- Reuse of specialist knowledge across projects and systems.
- Reduction in prototype dead ends and disconnected experiments.

## Questions This Persona Is Likely To Ask

- How do we preserve nuance when structuring domain expertise for AI systems?
- What does a governed path from prototype to production actually look like?
- How should evidence, rules, and edge cases be represented?
- How do we connect evaluation and revision back into the knowledge layer?

## Likely Objections

- Structuring expert knowledge may flatten important nuance.
- The governance layer could slow down experimentation.
- Not all research outputs are ready to be operationalised.
- The team may not have time to formalise knowledge while still exploring.

## Stakeholders This Persona Works With

- Research leadership
- Innovation teams
- Domain specialists
- Applied AI teams
- Technical programme leads
- Product, governance, and operational sponsors

## Proof This Persona Needs

- Examples where specialist knowledge became governed operational capability.
- Evidence that nuance, evidence, and edge cases can be preserved in structured forms.
- Practical patterns for moving from prototype to controlled deployment.

## Content That Helps At Each Stage

- Awareness: why high-value knowledge gets trapped in documents and pilots.
- Evaluation: what to capture, structure, and test first.
- Implementation: building the knowledge layer and connecting AICE safely.
- Scale: continuous evaluation, refinement, and reuse of domain assets.

## Source Feeds To Watch

- Research operations, knowledge management, and applied AI RSS feeds.
- Specialist domain and regulated-industry feeds where relevant.
- Evaluation, RAG, and knowledge-graph oriented newsletters.

## Reference Content To Reuse

- Past content on schema vs semantics, policy-as-graph, and what-is-IBOM.
- Use cases involving knowledge capture, regulation, and specialist lookup systems.
- Articles that resonated with readers looking for depth and domain credibility.

## Topics Most Likely To Perform

- Turning expert knowledge into machine-usable assets.
- Governed paths from prototype to production.
- Structured evidence, semantics, and linked data.
- Operationalising specialist domains safely.

## Engagement Signals To Watch

- Long dwell time on conceptually rich explainers.
- Stronger performance from depth-oriented content than short opinion pieces.
- Repeat visits to articles that connect semantics, schema, and operations.
- Higher engagement on content showing how nuance is preserved in structure.

## Preferred Formats

- Deep-dive explainers.
- Framework articles with conceptual diagrams.
- Use-case narratives from specialist domains.
- Q&A pieces tackling nuance, evidence, and evaluation.

## Editorial Voice

- Intellectually serious but still practical.
- Respectful of domain expertise.
- Clear about complexity without being inaccessible.
- More depth-first than hype-driven.

## Content Objectives

- Help expert readers trust that structure can preserve nuance.
- Show how governed systems emerge from knowledge-rich domains.
- Create authoritative content that supports serious evaluation, not shallow awareness only.

## CTA Patterns

- Begin with trapped knowledge or prototype failure patterns.
- Move into structure, evidence, and operationalisation logic.
- End with related use cases, resources, or role-specific next steps.

## Avoid Patterns

- Avoid shallow AI trend commentary.
- Do not imply every research output should become a production system.
- Avoid oversimplifying specialist domains for the sake of readability.

## Update Cadence

Review weekly for relevant domain signals and monthly for which depth-oriented topics and formats are sustaining engagement.

## Content That Helps

- Examples of specialist knowledge becoming governed systems.
- Guidance on evidence capture, structured formats, and linked datasets.
- Practical explanations of how AICE supports safe operationalisation.

## Messaging Notes

- Responds to depth, credibility, and domain fidelity.
- Needs reassurance that structure will not flatten specialist expertise.
- Cares about evaluation, evidence, and repeatable refinement.
